Top Countries to Study MBBS

Russia +
Studying MBBS in Russia is a popular choice for international students, particularly from India, due to its high-quality medical education at a relatively affordable cost, with most universities offering MBBS programs in English, making it accessible to non-Russian speakers; recognized by the WHO and NMC India, an MBBS degree from Russia allows graduates to practice medicine in India after passing the necessary screening test.
Uzbekistan +
Uzbekistan is considered a good option to study MBBS, with universities like Samarkand State Medical Institute, Tashkent Medical Academy, and Andijan State Medical Institute being popular choices for international students, offering quality medical education at a relatively affordable cost and recognized by organizations like WHO and MCI; making it an attractive destination for aspiring doctors seeking to study abroad.
Kazakhstan +
Kazakhstan is considered a good option to study MBBS due to its affordable tuition fees, high-quality medical education, and globally recognized universities like Astana Medical University, Semey State Medical University, and Al-Farabi Kazakh National University, which are all approved by the WHO and NMC making their degrees widely accepted internationally.
Philippines +
The Philippines is a popular destination for Indian students to study MBBS because of its high-quality medical education, English-taught programs, and reasonable costs
Georgia +
Georgia is considered a top destination for studying MBBS abroad. It has many medical universities, including prestigious schools and schools with international recognition.
Kyrgyzstan +
Kyrgyzstan is a popular destination for studying MBBS because of its affordable fees, high-quality medical education, and globally recognized degrees.
Egypt +
Egypt is considered a popular destination for studying MBBS, particularly for international students, due to its affordable tuition fees, well-regarded medical universities like Cairo University and Ain Shams University, quality clinical exposure in top hospitals, and a curriculum taught primarily in English, making it a cost-effective option for many aspiring doctors.
